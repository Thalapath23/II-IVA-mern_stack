Virat Kohli, born on November 5, 1988, in Delhi, is widely regarded as one of the greatest cricketers of all time. Known for his aggressive batting style, sharp cricketing mind, and unmatched consistency across formats, Kohli has redefined modern batting standards. He made his international debut in 2008 and quickly rose to prominence with his ability to chase down targets under pressure, earning him the title of “Chase Master.” Over the years, he has amassed more than 25,000 international runs, including 52 centuries in ODIs and 30 in Tests, placing him among the most prolific run-scorers in cricket history. As captain of the Indian team, he led India to numerous victories, including a historic Test series win in Australia in 2018–19. Beyond his records, Kohli is admired for his fitness revolution in Indian cricket, inspiring a generation of players to prioritize athleticism and discipline. Off the field, he is a global sports icon, brand ambassador, and philanthropist, while his marriage to Bollywood actress Anushka Sharma has made him one of the most recognized personalities in India. His journey reflects passion, resilience, and an unyielding drive to be the best, ensuring his legacy as a true legend of the game.
